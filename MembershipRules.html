Club Membership Rules

 

CLUB MEMBERSHIP RULES

(Approved at AGM 23rd July 2024)

The Club, being a non-profit making Organisation, lists its aims and objectives below;-

  1. To provide both League and Social Badminton for its Members within a Safe and Friendly Environment.
  2. To provide Coaching and Club facilities to the Students from the FitzWimarc School, and others.
  3. To support Equal Opportunities as defined in The Equality Act 2010 which requires the Club not to discriminate because of Disability, Race and Sexual Orientation.
  4. To support and adopt the Badminton England policies on ‘Equality and Diversity’, ‘Ethics and Conduct’, ‘Trans People’ and ‘The Protection and Safeguarding of Children and Vulnerable Adults’                            (see Rule 7.1 for details) 

  A copy of BADMINTON England’s Equality & Diversity policy is available on:  http://www.badmintonengland.co.uk/equality     

  1. Club Management
  • The Club will be Managed by an Executive Committee made up of the following Officers:
  •  Chairman,  Club Captain,  Fixture Secretary,  Secretary,   Treasurer,  Team Selectors,   ‘75’ Club Promoter,  Social Secretary,  Coaching & Development Officer /Assistant Social Secretary,  Coaching Officer,  Monday Night Club Representative,  Tuesday Night Club representative and a Welfare Officer.
  • Each Officer will be elected at the Annual General Meeting.
  • The Executive Committee will meet on at least four occasions each season and the proceedings will be recorded. Sub-Committees will be convened as necessary and will report back to the Main Committee.
  • At a Meeting of the Executive Committee six Members will form a quorum. At a Sub-Committee meeting four will form a quorum.
  • The property and assets of the Club will be vested in the Executive Committee.
  • The Executive Committee will have the power to suspend or expel any Member deemed guilty of conduct prejudicial to the good name of the Club.
  • The Executive Committee will, when required, have the power to fill vacancies on the Committee between AGM’s.
  • The Executive Committee will have the power to declare a seat vacant should a Member absent himself from three consecutive Committee Meetings without a satisfactory explanation.
  • The retiring Committee Members will be eligible for re-election at an AGM.
  • Nominations for Executive Committee Members will only be accepted from existing Playing Members and must be passed to the Secretary prior to the AGM in writing. No nominations will be accepted at an AGM unless supported by 75 per cent of those attending and voting at the AGM.
  1. Playing Membership 
  • Playing Membership will consist of Members who attend Monday and Tuesday club-nights 
  • It will be expected that players who participate in the Club’s League Teams should try and attend Tuesday Club nights. The Committee will have discretion to decide in this matter. 
  • Single Membership Fees will only allow attendance at one club night per week. Members wishing to attend both evenings will have an additional fee fixed by the Executive Committee. 
  • Candidates for Tuesday Playing Membership will be invited as a Guest until the Executive Committee decide whether they be accepted as a Permanent Club Member. 
  • Tuesday Club night playing Members should where possible make a commitment to play in the Club’s League Teams. If this commitment is not given the Executive Committee will still have the power to accept Membership.
  • The election of non-playing Honorary Members should be decided at an AGM. Honorary Members will be invited to attend all club events but shall have no voting rights at an AGM. 
  1. Annual General Meeting 
  • The AGM will be held at a date fixed by the Executive Committee each year. One-third of Playing Club membership will form a quorum.
  • The Secretary will give at least fourteen days notice of such meeting.
  • The end of the Club’s Financial will be 31st August each year.
  • Executive Officer reports will be received by the AGM Meeting, which will also elect the Executive Officers of the Committee, by ballot if necessary.
  • A special Extraordinary General Meeting may be called by the Executive Committee or by written request of at least ten
  1. Finance
  • Members who play in matches will be required to pay a Match fee. This sum to be agreed by the Executive Committee annually at the first Committee Meeting after the AGM.
  • All Monday and Tuesday Club-night Members will be required to pay an Annual Membership Fee towards Club expenses, plus the annual fee charged by Badminton England and Essex County BA, if applicable.

This fee will be fixed annually by the Executive Committee at their first committee meeting after the AGM.

(Monday & Tuesday club-night Membership fees can be paid in two half’s, 50% by 30th September and 50% by 31st January each year. The Badminton England & Essex County BA fee must be paid in full by 30th September)

Guest fees will also be fixed annually by the Executive Committee at their first meeting after the AGM.

4.3       Members in ‘full time education‘ will be entitled to a fifty per cent reduction in Annual Membership fee.

(There will be no reduction in Match fees)

4.4       All monies received must be regularly banked and Cheques must be signed by the following officers-: Treasurer, Chairman.

4.5       The Club shall run a ’75’ Club with a separate Bank Account being maintained.  All monies held must be regularly banked and Cheques may be signed by any of the following Officers:  Treasurer, Chairman and 75 Club Promoter.

Prize ratios, and the Rules of the ’75’ Club will be fixed by the Executive Committee taking into account the requirements of the Small Lotteries and Gaming Act.

Club Members who have elected to purchase shares in the ’75 Club’ will be expected to pay their donation in one sum along with their Annual Membership Fee on or before 30th September each year. All monies collected will be paid into the ’75 Club’ Account.

5 Team Selection

5.1 Team selection will be made by a Selection Committee that will be chaired by the Club’s appointed Team Selector and may include the nominated Team Captains, who will be appointed by the Executive Committee prior at the start of each playing season. 

5.2.      A selection report will be submitted by the Selectors to all Playing Members before the commencement of any new playing season and will include a list of all the matches to be played and the Players who will be expected to represent the club in these matches. 

5.3       The Team Selector/Match Secretary will report to each Executive Committee Meeting on the results of matches played and the Players who have played in these matches.

5.4       The Team Selector will have the discretion to call a Selection Meeting whenever it may become necessary.

  1. Alteration of Club Rules
  • No alteration to these rules will be made except at an Annual or Extraordinary General Meeting.
  • Notice of proposed alterations must be given in writing to the Secretary prior to the AGM.
  • All Club Members will be able to obtain a copy of the Clubs Rules from the Clubs Website: http://www.fitzwimarc.co.uk/.

Members will also, at the commencement of each playing season be supplied with a copy of these Rules as approved at an AGM.

7          Protection & Safeguarding of Young Children, Adults & Vulnerable Persons.

7.1       The Club takes seriously its responsibility for the Protection and Safeguarding of young Children, Adults and Vulnerable Persons in its care, as the welfare of these persons is paramount to their safety.

The Club’s appointed Executive Committee will ensure its Members and Coaches are made aware of how to identify signs of abuse. Members will also be made aware that the club has a Welfare Officer who is the clubs Responsible Officer for such matters and must be made aware of any abuse.

Who to contact:- Should any Club Member be concerned about the welfare of a young person, or in fact any member of the club, they should contact the Clubs Welfare Officer immediately, who will follow the Badminton England required procedures. These procedures require that all contacts made are to be dealt with using the utmost discretion and confidentially.

  • The Clubs Welfare Officer appointed in July 2015 is currently the Clubs Chairman Mr Michael Worship email:   worships@talktalk.net 
  • This Welfare Officer will act as the first point of contact for anyone, i.e. Club-Member, Coach, Volunteer, Parent or Child, who has a concern about poor practice or possible abuse.
  •  If anyone, as listed above, becomes aware of a possible abuse situation then they should document their concerns and seek immediate advice from the Clubs Welfare Officer, or the Local Social Services or Police, if the Welfare Officer is the subject of the allegation.
  • If the Welfare Officer cannot be contacted or is the subject of the allegation, then the person who has the concern about the young person’s welfare must act themselves.

8        Any other Business 

8.1       Any other matters not covered by these rules will be dealt with by the Club’s appointed Executive Committee.

8.2       At any Extraordinary or Annual General Meeting any Playing Member may vote by proxy, these being sent to the Secretary before the Meeting.

  • The Executive Committee shall ensure that the Clubs Web-site is regularly updated 

9          Dissolution 

9.1       In the event of the Dissolution of the Club, the assets of the Club, which include cash balances will be distributed equally amongst the ‘paying members’ listed, at the time of the dissolution.

Cash balances held at the time of dissolution, that represent donations received from Sponsors, will be returned to these Sponsors, before any equal distribution is made.
